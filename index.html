About the Workshop

Two Converging Challenges in Pervasive Computing

Pervasive computing environments — spanning IoT, wearables, smart homes, connected vehicles, edge/fog systems, and ambient intelligence platforms — continuously collect, process, and transmit sensitive personal and contextual data.

This raises two converging challenges: privacy preservation under severe resource constraints, and cryptographic agility against quantum threats. NIST's PQC standardization (ML-KEM, ML-DSA, SPHINCS+) provides quantum-resistant primitives, but their integration into constrained devices remains largely open.

PPT-PQC 2027 is the first PerCom-affiliated workshop to explicitly bridge these two areas through a pervasive-systems lens — addressing how systems can be simultaneously privacy-preserving and quantum-resilient without sacrificing usability, scalability, or energy efficiency.

Topics of Interest
  • Quantum-resilient cryptography for constrained devices
  • Post-quantum cryptographic engineering (ML-KEM, ML-DSA, SPHINCS+)
  • Differential privacy and federated learning on edge/IoT
  • Secure multiparty computation & homomorphic encryption
  • PQC/quantum security for wireless and mobile networks
  • Lightweight cryptography for embedded and pervasive systems
  • Hardware security for quantum-resilient devices
  • Privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs) for pervasive environments
  • PQC integration in smart grids, healthcare, and autonomous vehicles
  • Trustworthy AI intersecting PQC and quantum security
  • Anonymization and de-identification for ambient intelligence
  • Related applications and real-world deployment case studies

Paper Submission

Call for Papers

Submission Types

  • Full papers — original research contributions (IEEE 2-column format)
  • Short / WIP papers — work-in-progress and preliminary results
  • Posters & Demos — up to 5 accepted; ideal for student work

Expected Scope

We expect 15–18 full paper submissions and will accept approximately 8–10 (~40% acceptance rate, consistent with workshop norms). At least 20–30 participants including authors, TPC members, and walk-in attendees from the main conference.

Proceedings

Accepted papers will be published in the IEEE PerCom 2027 Workshop Proceedings (via IEEE Xplore). All accepted papers require at least one author registration for presentation.

Diversity & Inclusion

In line with IEEE PerCom's commitment, the organizing team actively pursues gender balance among speakers, panelists, and TPC members, and ensures geographical diversity in committee composition.
